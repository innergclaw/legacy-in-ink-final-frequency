## Verse

**Verse 6**

And the voice warned:

Everybody
does not deserve
thy time.

For the days are evil,
and time once poured out
does not return
because thou finally understood
where it was wasted.

Redeem the hours.

Redeem the conversations.

Redeem the mornings
spent explaining thyself
to people who came
only to drain the well.

Stop sitting
inside speech
that produces nothing.

Stop feeding
meaningless talk
with sacred breath.

Stop giving life
to ridiculous circles
that lead to no fruit,
no profit,
no wisdom,
no movement.

For some relationships
are not relationships.

They are leaks.

Some conversations
are not conversations.

They are traps
wearing familiar voices.

Some people
are not assigned
to thy future.

They are assigned
to keep thee rehearsing
the past.

Toxic company
will put a battleship
between thee
and success.

Toxic company
will pull static
between thee
and God.

Toxic company
will keep thee existing
when thou wast born
to live.

So observe.

Stay preserved.

Watch the fruit.

Watch the weight.

Watch who makes thee smaller
after every exchange.

Watch who leaves thy spirit
heavy
after calling it love.

Do not bless everybody
with conversation.

Do not bless everybody
with access.

Do not cast pearls
before those trained
to trample value
because they cannot recognize it.

Everybody
does not deserve thee.

Everybody
does not deserve
to be around thee.

Everybody
does not deserve
the opportunity
to say
they even knew thee.

This is the sixth principle:

Time is not casual.

Access is not cheap.

Conversation is not free.

Preserve thyself
from what keeps thee existing
when thy life
is calling thee
to live.

---

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about treating time, conversation, and access as sacred resources. Not every person deserves attention, explanation, proximity, or repeated chances to drain energy without producing meaning, fruit, or growth.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is wasting sacred time on toxic, empty, or unprofitable relationships. People often call it loyalty, history, or keeping peace, but the result is stagnation. The loop is broken by observing fruit, preserving energy, and refusing access to people who keep you existing instead of living.
