## Verse

**Verse 11**

And the question came:
How does a woman
avoid the wrong man?

How does she keep
from entering
the same room
with a different face?

And the voice answered:
Love thyself.
Value thyself.
Respect thyself
as woman,
as spirit,
as mind,
as creation.

Keep thy value
before thine eyes,
for once thou seest
thine own importance,
the hunger
begins to lose
its disguise.

Once life
reveals to thee
thy own individuality,
thy own capability,
thy own power
to become,
the capability
of another
will no longer feel
like survival.

It may be respected.
It may be honored.
It may enhance thee.
But it will not
become thy necessity.

For when thou discoverest
the beauty
of thine own infinite mind,
thou stop looking
for another body
to manufacture
what thy own soul
was built to carry.

Another person
may add to the light,
but they cannot be
the first candle.

Another person
may enhance the love,
but they cannot be
the original source
of what thou refusest
to give thyself.

Stop searching
for people
to create the feeling
that discipline,
healing,
self-respect,
and self-knowledge
must first create
within thee.

For many wander
into environments
full of self-hatred,
then ask hatred
to hold them gently.

They seek love
from those
who do not know
themselves.

They seek tenderness
from those
who are at war
inside their own name.

They seek protection
from those
who have not protected
their own spirit.

And if a person
hates himself,
if he does not
know himself,
or if he knows himself
and hates
what he sees,
then ask this:

How shall he give love
as shelter
when he has none
stored within?

How shall he share peace
when peace
has no home
inside him?

How shall he honor thee
when he has not yet learned
to honor the life
he carries?

This is the eleventh principle:
Do not borrow love
from empty places.

Know thyself
until another person
becomes addition,
not rescue.

Love thyself
until affection
must meet a standard
before it receives
access.

And value thyself
until the wrong man
can no longer
look like an answer.

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about self-love becoming discernment. It teaches that a person avoids the wrong relationship by first discovering their own value, capability, identity, and inner source. Love from another person should enhance what already exists, not create what is missing.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is looking for love, confidence, and emotional rescue from people who are still full of self-hatred or confusion. This verse exposes how dependency can make unhealthy people feel necessary. Once a person knows their own value, empty access loses power.

### Raw Theme

Women avoid the wrong man by knowing their own value and no longer depending on someone else to create love, confidence, or identity. A person who does not love themselves cannot become a healthy source of love for someone else.

### Principles

- Love yourself before seeking love as rescue.
- Another person can enhance the feeling, but should not create it.
- Knowing your own capability removes false necessity.
- Do not depend on self-hatred to give you love.
- Access should require self-respect and discernment.
- The wrong person loses power when your own value is clear.
