## Verse

**Verse 8**

And the voice came
with fire in the mouth:

Watch what thou sayest.

For the tongue
is not a toy.

It is a gate.

It can open
toward life.

It can open
toward death.

And many
have been speaking
as if words
do not carry keys.

They call destruction
by name
and then act surprised
when it answers.

They speak death
over their own days.

They rehearse endings.

They dress despair
in image.

They make sadness
look like style.

They invite darkness
for attention,
not understanding
that the wrong attention
can still arrive.

So the warning was given:

Stop speaking
against thy own breath.

Stop making agreements
with the grave
just to look real
in front of people
who cannot save thee.

If death is appointed,
it does not need
thy advertisement.

If trouble is coming,
it does not need
thy invitation.

If judgment is near,
do not hand it
directions
from thy own mouth.

Speak life.

Speak property.

Speak increase.

Speak the mother
being moved
out of poverty.

Speak the family
being carried
out of the old environment.

Speak currency
with purpose.

Speak the house.

Speak the business.

Speak the children
being covered
because thou chose
to remain here.

For life
is not corny.

Gratitude
is not weakness.

Hope
is not softness.

To celebrate breath
while blood
still warms the vein
is a holy discipline.

And guard the ear also.

For every conversation
is a deposit.

Some people deposit gossip.

Some deposit self-hate.

Some deposit fear.

Some deposit death
until the listener
becomes a container
for what was never his.

Do not become
a garbage can
for negative energy.

Do not let corrupt speech
make a home
inside thy spirit.

Watch what thou sayest.

Watch whom thou hearest.

Watch what thou eatest.

Watch the music
that enters thee
disguised as rhythm
while teaching the soul
to love destruction.

Separate from the voice
that is not reaching
for life.

Separate from the mouth
that only knows
how to bury futures.

For the children
need thee.

The assignment
needs thee.

The next page
needs thee.

This is the eighth principle:

Life and death
both listen
for permission.

Do not waste another sentence
feeding death.

Use thy ability
to the best
of thy ability.

Make it thy responsibility
to celebrate life.

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about speech as spiritual and practical programming. Words shape attention, identity, expectation, and direction. The message is not to pretend pain does not exist, but to stop rehearsing death, despair, and destruction as if they are the only proof of being real.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is using negative speech, death-talk, gossip, depressive content, and destructive music as identity. People repeat darkness for image, attention, or belonging until the language becomes an agreement with the very thing they claim they do not want. The loop is broken by guarding the mouth, guarding the ear, choosing life-language, and separating from conversations that deposit poison.

### Raw Theme

Watch what you say, what you listen to, and who you let speak into your life. Stop making death, negativity, depression, and destruction part of your image. Speak about life, family, property, growth, success, gratitude, and purpose instead.

### Principles

- Words are gates; they open direction.
- Do not speak death over your own life for image or attention.
- Negative conversations become deposits in the spirit.
- Guard what you say, hear, consume, and repeat.
- Celebrate life while breath is still present.
- Separate from voices that are not moving toward life.
