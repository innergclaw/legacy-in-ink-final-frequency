## Verse

**Verse 5**

And the voice admitted:

Yes,
the feelings be hurt.

Let no man lie
and call numbness strength.

Let no woman wound thee
and make thee pretend
the cut did not enter.

The heart can shake.

The stomach can turn.

The mind can replay
what the mouth refuses to say.

But hear this:

Pain is not permission
to abandon thyself.

Love is not a license
for disrespect.

Desire is not a reason
to let someone play
in thy face.

For there is one person
appointed to stand
on thy morals.

One person
appointed to guard
thy principles.

One person
appointed to keep respect
alive in thy own house.

And that person is thee.

So if the one thou likest
tries to turn affection
into access,

walk.

If the one thou lovest
tries to make loyalty
mean tolerating shame,

leave.

If the face is beautiful
but the spirit plays games
with thy dignity,

go.

And let the feelings hurt
while thy feet move.

Let the chest ache
while thy standards remain.

Let the tears come
without letting the boundary fall.

For feelings heal.

But self-betrayal
becomes memory in the body.

Feelings heal.

But disrespect tolerated once
will often return
wearing a new outfit.

Feelings heal.

But the soul remembers
the day thou chose attachment
over thy own name.

This is the fifth principle:

Never let pain
make thee negotiate
with dishonor.

Walk away hurt
before thou stay
and become less than thyself.

---

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about emotional honesty without self-abandonment. It allows pain to be real, but refuses to let hurt feelings become an excuse to tolerate disrespect, games, or treatment that violates personal principles.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is confusing love with permission. People often stay because they care, even when the situation is damaging their self-respect. The loop is broken by walking away while still hurt, trusting that feelings can heal faster than the damage caused by repeated self-betrayal.

### Raw Theme

Being hurt by someone you like or love is real, but the responsibility to stand on morals, principles, and self-respect still belongs to you. It is better to walk away with hurt feelings than stay and let someone play in your face.

### Principles

- Feelings can be hurt without principles being abandoned.
- Love does not excuse disrespect.
- Self-respect has to be guarded by the individual first.
- Walk away hurt before staying in dishonor.
- Feelings heal, but self-betrayal leaves residue.
- Standards matter most when attachment is strongest.

This belongs in Legacy In Ink because trauma loops often teach people to confuse attachment with love and tolerance with loyalty. The final frequency teaches emotional truth with boundaries: feel the pain, name the hurt, but do not surrender the principle.
