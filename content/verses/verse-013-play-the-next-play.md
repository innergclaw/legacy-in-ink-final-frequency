## Verse

**Verse 13**

And the voice said:
Get rid of
what thou thinkest
should happen.

Get rid of
the script
thou keepest writing
before the moment
has even spoken.

For one man says:
This should do that.

Another says:
That should do this.

Another waits
for her,
for him,
for them,
for the world
to move
according to the picture
inside his own head.

But there is
no such law.

There is only
what happens
in the moment.

There is only
the play
that appears
in front of thee.

There is only
the evidence
arriving in real time.

The wise man
may have a plan.

He may carry
a game plan,
an outline,
a playbook,
a picture
of what he believes
he may see.

But he does not
worship the plan.

He watches
what is actually
on the field.

For great coaches
do not marry
their expectations.

They adjust
when the game
reveals itself.

They do not cry
because the play
changed shape.

They do not bleed
their emotions
into what should
have happened.

They read.
They respond.
They move.

Play the game.

Play by play.

Next play.

Do not believe
that possession
means promise.

Do not believe
that because
the ball is
in thine hands,
the touchdown
is owed to thee.

Nothing is owed
until the play
is completed.

So remove
the fantasy
from the field.

Remove
the entitlement
from the moment.

Remove
the emotional debt
thou placed
on what never
signed an agreement.

Then see
what is here.

Then adjust
to what is real.

Then play
the next play.

---

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

Do not build your life around what you think should happen. Move from real evidence, not fantasy, entitlement, or emotional expectation.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is confusing a plan with reality. People get stuck because they emotionally attach to an imagined outcome, then cannot adjust when the real moment asks for discipline. The loop breaks when you read the situation clearly and respond play by play.
