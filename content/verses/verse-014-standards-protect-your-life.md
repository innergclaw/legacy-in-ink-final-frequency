## Verse

**Verse 14**

And it was said:
Look upon thy life
and examine
thy standards.

Do not begin
with the world.

Do not begin
with the people
thou keepest blaming.

Begin with thyself.

Ask:
Where have I lowered
the gate?

Where have I allowed
access
without order?

Where have I complained
about what enters,
while refusing
to protect
what is sacred?

For a man
without standards
becomes an open door.

And an open door
cannot choose
what walks in.

If thy appetite
has no standard,
thy body
will testify.

If thy circle
has no standard,
thy spirit
will be drained.

If thy attention
has no standard,
thy mind
will be occupied
by anything loud enough
to enter.

If thy desire
has no standard,
thou wilt call
every invitation
an opportunity.

And then wonder
why life keeps
delivering
what does not
honor thee.

Standards
better the life.

Standards
shape the body.

Standards
clean the room.

Standards
guard the gate.

Standards
teach the world
how to approach thee.

For once thou
treatest thyself
as valuable,
thou canst no longer
sit comfortably
among things
that reduce thee.

Once thou
becomest
the best definition
of thy own value,
thou wilt carry thyself
with different weight.

Thou wilt abstain
from what weakens thee.

Thou wilt eat
with intention.

Thou wilt speak
with intention.

Thou wilt choose
with intention.

Thou wilt stop
devaluing thyself
by standing
too long
beside people
who are not profitable
to thy spirit,
thy purpose,
or thy becoming.

For when the flood comes,
the standard rises.

When confusion comes,
the standard rises.

When temptation comes,
the standard rises.

When the enemy
seeks entry,
the standard
stands at the gate.

Standards
are not punishment.

Standards
are protection.

Standards
are maintenance.

Standards
are the fence
around the life
thou claimest
to be building.

---

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

Standards are how you protect your value. They are not just preferences; they are boundaries, disciplines, and filters that decide what can enter your life.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is living with open access, then blaming life for what comes through the door. Without standards, people confuse attention with value, access with opportunity, and comfort with safety. The loop breaks through self-examination and enforced boundaries.
