## Verse

**Verse 9**

And the voice turned
toward the daughters,
toward the women,
toward the heart
that keeps mistaking hunger
for love.

It said:

Value thyself.

For thou keepest giving
valuable things
to people
who have not proven
they carry value
for thee.

Thy time
is valuable.

Thy body
is valuable.

Thy heart
is valuable.

Thy passion,
thy softness,
thy attention,
thy help,
thy money,
thy ear,
thy patience,
thy belief -

all valuable.

So why give treasure
to the one
who only came
to take inventory?

Why pour love
into a vessel
that does not profess it
or possess it
for thee?

Why keep opening
the door
to the same spirit
wearing a different face?

For many
do not repeat pain
because they are foolish.

They repeat pain
because they are love-starved.

They hear
"I love you"
and call it proof.

They hear
"I want you"
and call it purpose.

They hear
"I need you"
and call it destiny.

But sometimes
it is only a song.

The same song.

The same hook.

The same script.

The same movie
called
Going Nowhere Fast
with new actors.

Old lies
from new mouths.

New bodies,
same bondage.

New attention,
same emptiness.

New promises,
same poverty
of spirit.

And until thou changest
thy own relationship
with thyself,
history will keep
finding thee available.

Until thou lovest thyself,
the wrong people
will keep sounding
convincing.

Until thou makest
daily deposits
of love
inside thy own soul,
anybody with a sweet lie
can overdraft thee.

This is not shame.

This is instruction.

Come up.

Not only in money,
but in standard.

Not only in appearance,
but in discernment.

Not only in being desired,
but in being protected
by thy own wisdom.

If the connection
adds no profit
to thy life,
measure it.

If the affection
requires self-abandonment,
reject it.

If the mouth says love
but the pattern
keeps stealing from thee,
believe the pattern.

This is the ninth principle:

Self-love
is not a caption.

It is a gatekeeper.

It decides
who gets access,
who gets silence,
who gets distance,
and who gets left
outside the next chapter.

Love thyself daily,
or old lies
will keep returning
with new mouths.

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about self-love as protection, especially for women who keep giving time, body, money, attention, and emotional labor to people who do not add real value. The message is not about shame; it is about recognizing worth before access is given away.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is repeating the same relationship wounds with different people because the inner standard has not changed. Love-starved people can mistake words for proof, attention for care, and desire for commitment. The loop is broken by making daily deposits of self-love, raising standards, and believing patterns over sweet language.
