## Verse

**Verse 1**

And the voice said:

Know thy value before the world names its price.

For a man who does not know what he carries
will trade gold for noise,
peace for approval,
and his name for a seat at a table
that never intended to feed him.

Let not hunger make thee cheap.

Let not loneliness make thee available.

Let not praise become the chain
that pulls thee away from thy own center.

For value is not proven by who desires thee.
Value is revealed by what cannot purchase thee.

And if they only honor thee when thou art useful,
then their love is labor,
and their loyalty is rent.

Stand still.

Count thyself before others count thee out.

Because the soul that forgets its worth
becomes easy to program,
easy to spend,
easy to pass down as a warning.

But the one who remembers says:

I am not here to be reduced.
I am not here to be consumed.
I am not here to be negotiated down
by people who never understood the cost
of becoming me.

This is the first principle:

Before they can respect the value,
you must stop discounting the vessel.

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

The clip theme points to self-valuation as a survival principle. If you do not know your own value, people, systems, relationships, and environments will assign one to you. The danger is not only being undervalued by others. The danger is agreeing with the lower price.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is internal discounting. When a person forgets their value, they become easier to use, easier to program, and easier to convince that crumbs are care. The loop breaks when worth is remembered before the world gets to rename it.
