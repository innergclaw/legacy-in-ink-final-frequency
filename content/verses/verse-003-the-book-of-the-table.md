## Verse

**Verse 3**

And the voice cried out:

Eat not loudly
in the land of hunger.

For if thou hast been blessed
to sit near the table,
then understand the eyes
of those still standing outside the door.

Abundance without awareness
becomes a signal.

And a signal sent into famine
will be read as an invitation.

Do not mistake the smiles.

Do not trust the laughter
of men who are watching thy plate
while their own hands remain empty.

For envy does not always announce itself.

Sometimes it claps.
Sometimes it jokes.
Sometimes it says congratulations
while measuring the distance
between thy neck and thy chain,
thy door and thy keys,
thy blessing and their need.

If thou hast bread,
be wise with it.

If thou hast increase,
move with covering.

If thou hast development
in a place where others have none,
then either become a blessing
or remove thyself from the altar of their hunger.

For the table was never meant
to become a stage.

And the blessing was never meant
to become bait.

There is a way to rise
without making starving men study thy height.

There is a way to win
without placing thy victory
in the mouth of predators.

There is a way to shine
without forgetting that some darkness
is not aesthetic.

It is appetite.

So the command is:

Do not perform prosperity
in front of pain
unless thou art prepared
to bring power into the room.

Share wisdom.
Share opportunity.
Share direction.
Or leave quietly.

Because the same room
that celebrates thy arrival
may become the room
that plans thy removal.

This is the third principle:

Do not confuse visibility with victory.

And do not eat like a king
in a room full of men
who have been trained by lack
to see every full plate
as prey.

## Reader Notes

### Core Message

This verse is about moving with discernment when you are blessed, visible, or elevated. If you are winning in an environment where others are hungry, broke, stagnant, or underdeveloped, you either need to empower the room or leave the room. Flexing without awareness can turn success into a target.

### Pattern Being Exposed

The pattern is public abundance without responsibility or protection. People often mistake visibility for status, but in survival environments, visibility can create danger. The loop is broken by learning when to share, when to build others up, and when to move quietly.
